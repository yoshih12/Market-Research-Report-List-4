Isotropic rubber magnets have a consistent magnetic field in all directions, making them ideal for simpler applications. Anisotropic rubber magnets have a preferred direction of magnetization, providing stronger magnetic properties and better performance for more demanding applications. Both types are cost-effective and easy to work with, making them popular choices for a wide range of magnetic solutions.</p></p>
