<p><p>Thermally conductive adhesives are materials used to bond components while efficiently transferring heat. The market is segmented into silicon-based and non-silicon-based types. Silicon-based adhesives offer flexibility, thermal stability, and excellent thermal conductivity, making them ideal for electronics and automotive applications. Non-silicon-based adhesives, often epoxy or acrylic, provide strong adhesion, higher rigidity, and better chemical resistance, suitable for industrial and high-performance applications. Both types cater to diverse thermal management needs, with silicon-based excelling in flexibility and non-silicon-based in durability and strength.</p></p>
