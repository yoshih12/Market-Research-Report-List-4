<p><p>Earth-fault detectors can be categorized into two primary types: Type I and Type II. Type I detectors are designed for low-impedance earthing systems, effectively identifying earth faults by monitoring current leakage and providing prompt fault detection. Type II detectors, on the other hand, are tailored for high-impedance earthing systems, focusing on voltage monitoring to detect earth faults that may not result in significant current leakage. Both types play a vital role in enhancing safety and reliability in electrical systems.</p></p>
