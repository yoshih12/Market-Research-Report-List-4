<p><p>An arterial stent is a small, mesh-like tube made of metal or polymer that is inserted into narrowed or blocked arteries to keep them open, facilitating blood flow. These devices are commonly used in procedures such as angioplasty to treat conditions like coronary artery disease, peripheral artery disease, and carotid artery stenosis. The increasing prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, coupled with advancements in stent technology, is driving the growth of the arterial stent market.</p><p>Market analysis indicates a robust expansion, as healthcare systems are increasingly adopting minimally invasive procedures. The arterial stent market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 11.70% during the forecast period. Emerging trends include the development of drug-eluting stents, which release medication to prevent restenosis, and bioabsorbable stents that provide temporary support before being absorbed by the body. Additionally, the rising geriatric population and lifestyle-related risk factors are expected to boost demand. Continuous research and innovation, focused on enhancing the efficacy and safety profiles of stents, will further shape market dynamics. As regulatory approvals for advanced stent designs increase, the market is poised for significant growth in the coming years.</p></p>

<p><p>The arterial stent market comprises primarily two types: coronary stents and peripheral stents. Coronary stents are designed to support arteries in the heart, preventing blockages and ensuring proper blood flow. They are crucial in treating coronary artery disease. Peripheral stents, on the other hand, target arteries outside the heart, such as those in the legs or arms, addressing vascular issues like peripheral artery disease. Both types enhance patient outcomes by improving circulation and reducing the risk of serious complications.</p></p>
