There are five main types of DNA polymerases in prokaryotes: Pol I, Pol II, Pol III, Pol IV, and Pol V. Each of these polymerases plays a specific role in DNA replication and repair. Pol I is involved in DNA repair and processing of Okazaki fragments. Pol II is involved in DNA repair mechanisms. Pol III is the main enzyme responsible for DNA replication. Pol IV and Pol V are involved in DNA damage tolerance.</p></p>
