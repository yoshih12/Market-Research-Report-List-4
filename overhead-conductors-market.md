<p><p>The overhead conductors market is primarily categorized into two types: conventional conductors and high-temperature conductors. Conventional conductors, typically made of aluminum or copper, are widely used for their reliability and cost-effectiveness in standard transmission applications. In contrast, high-temperature conductors are designed to operate at elevated temperatures, allowing for increased current-carrying capacity and reduced line sag. They are often constructed from advanced materials like aluminum alloys and have enhanced performance characteristics, making them suitable for modern power transmission needs.</p></p>
