<p><p>The microfluidic biosensors market comprises two main types: electrochemical sensors and optical sensors. Electrochemical sensors detect biochemical reactions by measuring changes in voltage or current, making them suitable for real-time monitoring of various analytes. Optical sensors, on the other hand, rely on light interaction with biological molecules to detect changes, offering high sensitivity and specificity for applications like diagnostics and environmental monitoring. Both types leverage microfluidic technology to enable miniaturized and efficient analysis, catering to diverse clinical and industrial needs.</p></p>
