In the market, there are two main types of switches: leaf switches and spine switches. Leaf switches handle connections to endpoints, while spine switches provide high-bandwidth connections between leaf switches. Leaf switches are typically cheaper and easier to deploy, making them suitable for access layer switches. Spine switches are more powerful and are typically used in the data center core for high-performance networking.</p></p>
