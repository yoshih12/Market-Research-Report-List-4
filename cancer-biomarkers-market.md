<p><p>The cancer biomarkers market encompasses two primary types: protein biomarkers and genetic biomarkers. Protein biomarkers are specific proteins associated with cancer presence or progression, often used for diagnosis, prognosis, and treatment monitoring. Genetic biomarkers, on the other hand, involve alterations in DNA sequences or gene expression patterns that can indicate cancer susceptibility or response to therapies. Both types play critical roles in personalizing cancer treatment, enabling targeted therapies, and improving patient outcomes through more precise diagnosis and management strategies.</p></p>
