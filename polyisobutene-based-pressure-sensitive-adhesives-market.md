<p><p>Polyisobutene-based pressure-sensitive adhesives (PSAs) are categorized into two main types: waterborne and solvent-borne. Waterborne PSAs utilize water as the primary solvent, making them more environmentally friendly with reduced volatile organic compounds (VOCs). They provide good adhesion and flexibility suitable for various applications. In contrast, solvent-borne PSAs use organic solvents, offering strong bonding performance and durability, often favored in industrial settings. Both types are crucial for applications in packaging, automotive, and electronics, balancing performance with environmental considerations.</p></p>
