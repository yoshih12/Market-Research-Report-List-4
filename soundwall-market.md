<p><p>Ground-mounted noise barriers are typically installed on the ground between the noise source and the receiver to reduce noise levels. They are often made of materials such as concrete, wood, or metal. On the other hand, structure-mounted noise barriers are attached to existing structures, such as buildings or bridges, for noise reduction. These barriers can be made of various materials and are designed to be installed vertically or horizontally depending on the specific needs of the location.</p></p>
