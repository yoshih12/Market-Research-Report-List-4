<p><p>Inflammatory markers are molecules produced in response to inflammation in the body. There are several types of inflammatory markers, including Interleukin, C Reactive Protein, Cellular Adhesion Molecule, Tumor Necrosis Factor-α, and others. Interleukins are signaling proteins that regulate immune response. C Reactive Protein is a marker of systemic inflammation. Cellular Adhesion Molecules are involved in the attachment of immune cells to the site of inflammation. Tumor Necrosis Factor-α is involved in regulating immune cells and inflammation. Other inflammatory markers play various roles in the inflammatory response in the body.</p></p>
