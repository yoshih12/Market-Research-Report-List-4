<p><p>The Open Angle Glaucoma Drugs Market consists of various types of medications including Prostaglandins, Carbonic Anhydrase Inhibitors, Cholinergic Drugs, and Others. Prostaglandins work by increasing the outflow of fluid from the eye, reducing intraocular pressure. Carbonic Anhydrase Inhibitors also reduce intraocular pressure by decreasing the production of fluid in the eye. Cholinergic Drugs work by increasing the drainage of fluid from the eye. Other types of medications may include alpha agonists, beta blockers, and combination drugs that target multiple pathways to lower intraocular pressure in patients with open-angle glaucoma.</p></p>
