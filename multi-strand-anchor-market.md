<p><ul><li>Tensile</li><li>Compressive</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Multi-strand anchor market types include tensile and compressive anchors, each designed for different load requirements. Tensile anchors are used to resist pulling forces, making them ideal for applications where uplift is a concern, such as in bridges or tall structures. Compressive anchors, on the other hand, are intended to withstand compressive forces, often utilized in foundations and heavy structures. Both anchor types leverage multiple strands of material to enhance strength and reliability, adapting to varying environmental and structural demands.</p></p>
