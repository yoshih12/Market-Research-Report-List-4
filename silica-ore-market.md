<p><ul><li>Quartz</li><li>Tridymite</li><li>Cristobalite</li><li>Coesite</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The silica ore market is primarily categorized into four types: quartz, tridymite, cristobalite, and coesite. Quartz, the most abundant form, is widely used in glass, ceramics, and silicon production. Tridymite and cristobalite are high-temperature stable forms used in specialized glass and ceramics, offering enhanced thermal properties. Coesite, a high-pressure variant, is utilized in advanced applications such as abrasives and geological studies. Each type features unique physical and chemical properties, catering to diverse industrial needs.</p></p>
