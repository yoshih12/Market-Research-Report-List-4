They operate by emitting sonar waves from a towed or mounted device, which then bounce off the seabed and return to the system, enabling the generation of detailed imagery of the underwater environment. <p><ul><li>Single-beam</li><li>Multi-beam</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The side scan sonar market is primarily divided into single-beam and multi-beam systems. Single-beam side scan sonars utilize one sonar channel to produce high-resolution images of the seafloor, making them suitable for shallow waters and specific targets. In contrast, multi-beam side scan sonars employ multiple beams, allowing for broader coverage and detailed mapping of larger areas, ideal for deep-water exploration and habitat monitoring. Both types serve essential roles in marine research, underwater archaeology, and geological studies.</p></p>
