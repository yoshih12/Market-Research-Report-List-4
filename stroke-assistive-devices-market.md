<p><p>Stroke assistive devices market can be categorized into two types: mobility assistive devices and fixed assistive devices. Mobility assistive devices help stroke survivors with walking, transferring, and mobility issues. These devices include canes, walkers, wheelchairs, and scooters. On the other hand, fixed assistive devices are designed to help with tasks that require more precision and stability such as eating, dressing, grooming, and bathing. These devices include grab bars, adaptive utensils, dressing aids, and raised toilet seats.</p></p>
